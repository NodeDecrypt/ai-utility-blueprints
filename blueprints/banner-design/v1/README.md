# Banner Design

Create a banner image from brand, product, and design inputs.

---

## Summary

This blueprint helps a human runner turn basic brand and product information into a finished banner image through a simple step-by-step process.

It is written for manual use in Node Decrypt Community and does not require knowledge of internal platform concepts.

---

## Who this is for

This blueprint is useful for:

- ecommerce sellers
- marketers
- beginner creators
- small business owners
- designers who want a repeatable flow

---

## Final deliverable

This blueprint produces:

- final banner image

---

## Tools used

This blueprint uses the following tools:

- `business_brief_chatbot` `v1`
- `design_brief_chatbot` `v1`
- `design_specs_chatbot` `v1`
- `google_flow` `v1`

---

## Asset checklist

Before starting, prepare these assets if available:

- brand name
- logo image
- product images
- design reference images
- headline
- subheadline
- platform
- layout ratio

---

## Step summary

### Step 1 — Create Requirement Summary

Turn the raw business and design information into a clear requirement summary.

### Step 2 — Create Professional Design Brief

Turn the requirement summary into a stronger and more professional creative brief.

### Step 3 — Create Design Specs

Turn the professional design brief into detailed design specs for generation.

### Step 4 — Generate Final Banner Image

Use the final design specs and visual assets to create the final banner image.

---

## What this blueprint is good for

This blueprint is good when you want:

- a clearer design workflow
- better prompt quality before generation
- a more structured manual process
- more consistency between idea and final banner

---

## Limitations

This blueprint does not include:

- internal automation setup
- direct tool URLs
- secret prompts
- private selectors
- API keys
- internal-only Node Decrypt configuration

The quality of the final result still depends on:

- the quality of the provided product images
- the clarity of the text inputs
- the suitability of the design direction
- the quality of the tool outputs
